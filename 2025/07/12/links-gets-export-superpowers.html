Five export formats. Zero vendor lock-in. Complete data freedom.

Digital preservation isn’t just about saving content. It’s about maintaining access and control. Links embraces complete data freedom - your content should work everywhere.

HTML Export - Browser Migration Made Easy
The universal standard. Every major browser imports HTML bookmark files. Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge - they all speak this language. Moving from Links to your browser’s bookmark manager? One click export, one click import.

CSV Export - Data Analysis Ready
Spreadsheet-friendly format with comprehensive metadata. URLs, titles, save dates, file sizes, AI summaries - everything in neat columns. Perfect for analysis, reporting, or integration with other tools.

BAR Export - Complete Offline Archive
Creates a complete, offline-browsable archive of your entire collection. All your saved content, all your metadata, packaged into a single downloadable file. Internet goes down? Server crashes? You still have everything, fully accessible offline.

Markdown Export - Knowledge System Integration
Each bookmark becomes a clean Markdown file with metadata headers and AI-generated summaries. Import directly into Obsidian, Notion, Logseq, or any Markdown-based knowledge system. Your archived links become part of your personal knowledge graph.

JSON Export - Full System Backup
Every piece of data Links knows about your collection, structured for programmatic access. Perfect for backup, migration, or building your own tools. Nothing gets lost, nothing gets simplified.

This isn’t just convenience. It’s digital freedom. Too many tools trap your data with proprietary formats. Links takes the opposite approach - multiple export formats mean multiple escape routes. Your investment is protected.

Export functionality is live at links.alexanderkucera.de right now. Choose your format, click export, download your file. Your digital collection just became a lot more portable.
